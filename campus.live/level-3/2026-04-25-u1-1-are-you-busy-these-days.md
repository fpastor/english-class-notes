## Everyday activities (1/2)

Read about the use of the **Simple Present Tense** in the affirmative form

This verb tense is used to express habits, general truths, facts, repeated actions, unchanging situations, emotions, senses, etc.  
  
* I **go** to the office from Monday to Friday.
* I hardly ever **work** on the weekends.
* I **live** in Miami.
* I **love** Miami!
  
In the affirmative, the third person singular (He, She, It) adds an **-s / -es / -ies** to the verb in the affirmative form:

Read the same examples but with the third person singular:

* He go**es** to the office from Monday to Friday.
* He hardly ever work**s** on the weekends.

## Everyday activities (2/2)

An **auxiliary verb** is needed in the **negative and interrogative forms.**
  
Use **do** when the subject is: I, You, We, They.
  
* I **don't work** on the weekends.
* ***Do** you **work** on the weekends?
  
Use **does** when the subject is: He, She, It.
  
* He **doesn't work** on the weekends
* ***Does** she **work** on the weekends?

## Simple Present Tense - review

We use the Simple Present Tense for habitual or permanent activities, and for things that are usually true. We also use this tense for schedules and timetables.

| Affirmative                | Negative                          | Interrogative                  |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| I live in Miami.           | I don't live in Miami.            | Do I live in Miami?            |
| You work for IBM.          | You don't work for IBM.           | Do you work for IBM?           |
| He / She / It lives in NY. | He / She / It doesn't live in NY. | Does he / she / it live in NY? |
| They work for Coca-Cola.   | They don't work for Coca-Cola.    | Do they work for Coca-Cola?    |

## Current activities

==Present Continuous Tense==

We use this tense to **show that something is happening at a certain moment in time.**

To form this tense we use **am/is/are + verb -ing**

* I'**m working** late this week.
* She'**s having** a conference call later today.
* They'**re going** abroad.

## Present Continuous Tense - review

This tense is referred to as progressive tense as it is used to describe actions in progress, either at the moment of speaking or in the future.

| Affirmative             | Negative                            | Interrogative                 |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| I am writing a report.  | I am not having lunch.              | Am I paying attention?        |
| You are working hard.   | You are not answering my calls.     | Are you working on a project? |
| She is giving a speech. | She is not addressing the audience? | Is she felling well?          |

## Special cases (1/2)

Sometimes some verbs cannot be used in the continuous form, even if they refer to temporary situations. These are called **stative verbs.**
  
==I'm sorry, I don't understand.== **NOT:** ~~I'm sorry I'm not understanding.~~
  
### Stative verbs often refer to thoughts and opinions.

==She doesn't know what to do.== **NOT:** ~~She isn't knowing what to do.~~
==Do you agree with me?==
  
Other verbs in this group include: **believe, doubt, guess, imagine, mean, remember, think...**
  
### Other stative verbs relate to feelings and emotions.

==I love this song. Who sings it?== **NOT:** ~~I'm loving this song.~~
==What do you want to do now?==  
==I hate my new boss!==  
  
Other stative verbs in this group include: **dislike, love, prefer, want, wish...**
  
> **Note:** although **enjoy** is a verb of emotion, it is used in the continuous tense (as an action). ==I'm enjoying the party.==

## Special cases (2/2)

**Hear, feel, see, taste, smell** are verbs that describe the senses.

These verbs aren't usually used in the continuous forms. They are often used with **can.**

==It smells of smoke in here.== **NOT:** ~~It's smelling of smoke in here.~~
==I can't see anything. It's too dark.==

### Stative verbs describe things that are not actions.  

Look at these two sentences carefully:

* I **think** we should go to Croatia for our vacation this year.
* Sorry, what did you say? I was **thinking** about my vacation.

The first sentence ==THINK== is used to express an **opinion**, but in the second sentence ==THINK== expresses an **action.**

## Stative verbs in everyday speech

Stative verbs, while not grammatically correct, are often used in common everyday speech.
  
It would be very common to hear someone say: **thanking you, I'm loving this song!, I'm doubting she's correct, or I'm hating my boss, right now.**
  
In everyday language, it adds emphasis to the verb and implies momentary sentiment.

## Every day or now?

Read and choose the correct option. You will have to consider the type of action/state in each sentence. Remember: "I work" is a permanent or everyday action, but "I am working" is a transitory action taking place now, at present, or at the moment of speaking.

* Carlos usually ==works== at the main office, but today he ==is working== at home.
* What time  ==do you leave== home to get to work everyday?
* Look at the woman over there. Why ==is she working== at John's desk?
* They ==are having== huge problems with the elevators nowadays.
* What ==are you working on== at the moment? Anything interesting?

## Do you work in Munich?

Put the conversation in order.

* **A:** What do you do?
* **B:** I'm an electrical engineer for Siemens.
* **A:** Really? Here in Munich?
* **B:** That's right. Do you know Munich?
* **A:** Oh, yes, great city. So, how are you enjoying the conference so far?
