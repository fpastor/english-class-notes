# U1.2 Everyday and current activities

In this lesson you will differentiate everyday from current activities, practice how to make direct questions, and distinguish up (rise) or down (fall) intonation.

|Habitual / permanent actions|Current / transitory actions|Intonation in questions|

## Interviews

What you have just read is a type of conversation. But this is not an ordinary one; this is an **interview**. Generally, the objective of an interview is to find out some information. And we ask **questions** to get missing information.  
  
There are different types of questions you can ask:  
  
**Yes/No Questions:** We use them if we want to ==check information.==

* Do you travel to work by train everyday?
* Are you reading that book now?

**Wh- Questions:** We use them if we need to ==get information== rather than check it.

* What do you usually do while you're commuting?
* Where are you going?

> **NOTE:** questions about a subject (who? what?) have their own special structure; they do not require an auxiliary verb, we replace the subject with the question word.
> **Who works** in IT? *Software developers.*
> **What's that** room? *A meeting room.*

## Auxiliary verbs and follow-up questions

Auxiliary verbs (do, have, be and modals) are used:  
  
**To form questions and negatives  **

* We don't have any subsidiaries.  
  
**To ask follow-up questions to show surprise.  **

* He doesn't have any formal qualifications. Doesn't he?  
  
**To form question tags  **

* He doesn't speak French, does he? *(negative sentence + positive tag)*
* They're working today, aren't they? *(positive sentence + negative tag)*
* We can't attend the meeting tomorrow, can we? *(negative sentence + positive tag)*

## Intonation in questions

**Listen to the examples.**

Notice that we use a different intonation for each question:

**Rising intonation:** The voice goes up. We use it to show politeness.

* *Do you enjoy your **job**?**

**Falling intonation:** The voice goes down. We use it to ask and give information.

* *Where are you **staying** in Rio?*
