## How much do people suffer from stress?

Read the following article about different stressful situations at work and how they affect people.

**Stress at work.**
  
Over the past decades, complaints about the rising levels of stress in the working environment have increased dramatically. There are many situations that make people feel stressed, and we're going to discuss the most repeated ones.

First, 90% of workers have stated that bosses and managers are one of the main sources of stress. They say they suffer every time they have to confront their bosses about work decisions, asking for raises, or even sharing their opinions.

Then, 75% have chosen coworkers as a source of stress. Apparently, coworkers who are aggressive, impolite, and even excessively friendly make people nervous.

Finally, 40% have declared that their jobs are stressful. These people include doctors, dentists, police officers, teachers, fire fighters, engineers, and sales people. Effects of stress can be physical (headaches, backaches, being over-tired, etc.) or psychological (depression, aggressiveness, or isolation).

## More information, please!

When we want to know more about that experience, we can use the Simple Past Tense.  
  
- I've skydived many times.
- When **was** the first time you skydived?
- Oh, it **was** three years ago.
- And, **did** you **like** it?
- Oh, yes. It **was** excellent.

- Have you ever been to Europe?
- Yes, I have.
- When **did** you **go**?
- I **went** in 1998.
